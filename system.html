Explanation
What it is

Side control is a dominant ground position in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu where you pin your opponent to the mat from the side, with your chest pressing against theirs. Your hips are low, your weight is distributed to maximize pressure, and your arms control their head and hip.

When to use it

You transition to side control after passing the guard, sweeping from bottom, or taking down your opponent. It's a consolidation position -- a place to rest, plan your next attack, and wear down your opponent with pressure before advancing to mount or setting up submissions.

How it works

The key mechanics are weight distribution (chest-to-chest pressure), hip positioning (hips low and angled), and arm control (crossface and underhook). You want to deny your opponent the ability to create frames or recover guard by staying heavy and controlling their movement.
